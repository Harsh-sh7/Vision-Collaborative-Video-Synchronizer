# Vision

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term vision of Universal Watch Party is to become the universal collaboration platform for shared digital media experiences.

Rather than focusing on a single streaming provider or a specific media type, the platform aims to support a broad ecosystem of online and offline content while maintaining a consistent interaction model.

The product should eventually support:
* Entertainment
* Education
* Business collaboration
* Online classrooms
* Presentations
* Training sessions
* Community events
* Remote family experiences

---

## Product Identity

The platform is not intended to replace video conferencing software. It is also not intended to replace streaming providers.

Instead, it complements existing content platforms by providing synchronization and collaboration capabilities around legitimate media consumption.

---

## Core Experience

The desired user experience is simple:
1. A host creates a room.
2. Participants join.
3. Everyone watches together.

Communication happens naturally through integrated collaboration tools. The technology behind synchronization remains largely invisible to users.

---

## Design Goals

Every design decision should support one or more of the following goals:
* **Simplicity**: Low barrier to entry and easy setups.
* **Performance & Low Latency**: Fast responsiveness and low playback drift.
* **Reliability**: Resilient to disconnects, page reloads, and failures.
* **Accessibility**: Support for diverse groups of users.
* **Platform Independence**: Consistency across different websites and engines.
* **Scalability**: Ability to handle multiple rooms and users concurrently.
* **Extensibility**: Modular structure to easily add new playback engines.
* **User Privacy**: No DRM bypasses or unnecessary telemetry.
* **Minimal Bandwidth Usage**: Synchronizing states instead of streaming pixels.

---

## Future Direction

Future versions of the platform may expand into:
* Mobile devices, desktop applications, smart TVs, and gaming consoles.
* Advanced educational and enterprise collaboration tools.
* AI-powered moderation and AI-generated summaries.
* Shared whiteboards, live quizzes, interactive overlays, community rooms, and public events.

The underlying architecture should be designed to accommodate future expansion without requiring a major redesign.
